Margaret Hamilton

a tribute to the first female software engineer

Here is a timeline of Hamilton's accomplishments:

  • 1959 - Began working for Edward Norton Lorenz, in the meteorology department at MIT.
  • 1961 - Worked on the Semi-Automatic Ground Environment (SAGE) Project at the MIT Lincoln Lab
  • 1964 - Joined the Charles Stark Draper Laboratory at MIT, which worked on the Apollo Space Mission.
  • 1976 - Co-founded a company with Saydean Zeldin called Higher Order Software (HOS) to further develop ideas in error prevention and fault tolerance.
  • 1986 - Received the Augusta Ada Lovelace Award by the Association for Women in Computing.
  • 2003 - Given the NASA Exceptional Space Act Award for scientific and technical contributions.
  • 2009 - Received the Outstanding Alumni Award by Earlham College.
  • 2016 - Received the Presidential Medal of Freedom from Barack Obama
  • 2017 - Received the Computer History Museum Fellow Award, which honors exceptional men and women whose computing ideas have changed the world.
  • 2018 - Awarded an honorary doctorate degree by the Polytechnic University of Catalonia.
  • 2019 - Awarded an honorary doctorate degree by Bard College.
  • 2019 - Awarded the Intrepid Lifetime Achievement Award.

"Looking back, we were the luckiest people in the world. There was no choice but to be pioneers; no time to be beginners."

--Margaret H. Hamilton
